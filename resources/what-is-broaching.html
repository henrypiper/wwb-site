What Is Broaching?

Broaching is a precision machining process used to create internal or external profiles with exceptional accuracy, repeatability, and surface finish.

PRECISION CUTTING
Broaching removes material using a multi-tooth cutting tool that progressively shapes the final geometry in a single pass.
HIGH PRODUCTION
The process is ideal for high-volume manufacturing due to its speed, consistency, and repeatability.
COMPLEX GEOMETRIES
Broaching is commonly used to produce splines, keyways, hexes, serrations, and custom internal forms.
THE PROCESS

How Broaching Works

A broach tool contains a series of progressively larger cutting teeth. As the tool moves through or across the workpiece, each tooth removes a small amount of material until the final shape is achieved.

Because the cutting geometry is built directly into the broach tool, the process delivers excellent dimensional consistency and surface finish while minimizing secondary machining operations.

INTERNAL BROACHING
Used for producing internal splines, keyways, polygon shapes, and precision bores.
EXTERNAL BROACHING
Used to machine external forms, flats, slots, and complex outer profiles.
CUSTOM TOOLING
Specialized broach tooling can be designed for unique geometries and production requirements.
APPLICATIONS

Industries That Use Broaching

Broaching is widely used across aerospace, automotive, defense, firearms, heavy equipment, and industrial manufacturing applications.

The process is especially valuable for parts requiring precise internal geometries, high production efficiency, and repeatable dimensional accuracy.
